Why HOS Exists

Sudanese students are disconnected from one another. Sudanese professionals operate without shared infrastructure. Researchers work in isolation. Entrepreneurs lack networks. Diaspora organizations rarely cooperate.

The talent exists. The ambition exists. The institutions do not.

House of Sudan exists to build the institutions that connect them — across borders, disciplines, and generations.

About

Origins

House of Sudan emerged from a belief that Sudan's future requires institutions capable of connecting talent across borders. Not another student club. Not another diaspora association. An institution — built to last, built to matter.

Founded in New York, HOS is a Sudanese-American intellectual and leadership society focused on professional network-building, capital formation, and national development.

Ten-Year Vision

The largest Sudanese professional network in North America. A leading diaspora think tank with recognized publications. Entrepreneurship initiatives. Fellowships. Scholarships. An annual conference.

The first institution people turn to when they want Sudanese talent, research, policy analysis, or diaspora leadership.
