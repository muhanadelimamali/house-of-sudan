Subsidiary — I
Gazette
I

The Gazette is being built as the authoritative English-language publication on Sudanese affairs — covering politics, economics, culture, and diaspora with the depth and precision those subjects deserve.

Current coverage in Western media treats Sudan episodically, in moments of crisis, without continuity or context. The Gazette operates on a different logic: sustained editorial attention, original analysis, and a long institutional memory. The audience is informed professionals, academics, policymakers, and diaspora communities who require more than wire dispatches.

The long-term ambition is syndication and partnership with established media organizations, positioning the Gazette as an indispensable source rather than an alternative one. Policy Research outputs and Investment Research theses will find their public-facing expression here.

Subsidiary — II
Investment Research
II
Mission

To produce rigorous, decision-oriented investment research through transaction memos, financial models, and valuation analysis — connecting capital discipline with long-term economic development thinking.

Vision

To become a credible platform where high-quality investment analysis informs future capital deployment, institutional collaboration, and Sudan-focused reconstruction and development initiatives.

Organizational Overview
Who

HOS Investment Research is an internal initiative of House of Sudan, led by a Sudan-focused analyst producing institutional-style investment research and transaction analysis.

What

Full transaction memoranda, financial models, valuation analyses, and capital structure assessments across public companies and sectors, alongside select policy-adjacent work tied to economic development and infrastructure.

When

A structured, deal-by-deal cadence. Each project completed end-to-end: from research and modeling to written memoranda, internal review, and public one-page summaries.

Where

Based in the United States, operating digitally, with analysis spanning U.S. and global companies, infrastructure, and Sudan-relevant development themes.

Why

To build a disciplined body of investment work that connects policy thinking with real capital decision-making — establishing credibility, repeatability, and readiness for future Sudan-focused investment and advisory efforts.

Subsidiary — III
Policy Research
III

Policy design and analysis focused on governance, economic development, and institutional reform — with a specific mandate around Sudan's reconstruction and the diaspora's role in shaping it.

Outputs are structured for submission to think tanks, fellowship programs, and U.S. government advisory channels. The goal is durable influence at the institutional level, not visibility for its own sake. A policy memo that reaches a deputy assistant secretary matters more than a widely-shared editorial.

The Lab also serves as a training ground for Sudanese professionals building careers in policy and public service — developing the analytical frameworks and institutional relationships required to operate effectively in Washington, Brussels, and Nairobi. Sudan's reconstruction will require technically capable, politically connected advocates in every room where decisions are made.

Subsidiary — IV
Azrag Studio
IV

Azrag Studio is the cultural and creative arm of House of Sudan — a vehicle for Sudanese identity, narrative, and visual expression built to operate at the intersection of art and commerce.

The studio produces limited-edition goods tied to cultural moments, with direct-to-consumer distribution across the Northeast and Canada. Drops are intentional, scarce, and anchored in Sudanese visual heritage. The aesthetic draws from historical textiles, manuscript traditions, and the rich material culture of the Nile Valley.

Beyond product, Azrag is a platform for Sudanese creative professionals — a space where the visual language of the diaspora is developed with rigor and ambition rather than nostalgia. The studio is being positioned as a long-term brand with institutional legitimacy, not a seasonal project.

Subsidiary — V
Community Initiatives
V

Programs and initiatives focused on building strong, capable, and connected Sudanese communities across the diaspora — with particular attention to the coordination gaps that leave Sudanese talent underrepresented in high-income fields outside medicine.

The immediate priority is mentorship infrastructure: connecting established Sudanese professionals with students and early-career individuals navigating finance, law, consulting, and technology. These relationships are routine in well-networked communities; for Sudanese Americans they remain largely informal and accidental.

Longer term, Community Initiatives will develop the pipeline feeding the broader House of Sudan network: identifying talented young Sudanese Americans early, building their institutional confidence, and connecting them to the professional and intellectual infrastructure that accelerates careers. The ambition is generational.
